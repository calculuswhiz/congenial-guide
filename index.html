The Facts about Bitcoin IRA, Inc.

An in-depth analysis on why you should stay far away from this organization

Preface

When I first heard of Bitcoin IRA, Inc, it was when I was watching Youtube on my phone. "This has to be some kind of joke," I thought, but sadly, no, it wasn't. This is a real company that wants you to pay real money to buy an extremely volatile asset with your long-term investment using their services. And they're mass-advertising it to the general public.

But some questions were raised in my head. Namely:

  • Are they convincing people to do this? If so, how?
    • Who are they trying to market to?
    • What are they telling the general public?
    • What are they hiding?
    • What's in it for them?
  • And of course: Is it a ruse, and if so, how elaborate of a ruse is it?

Motivation

I am a value investor acolyte, which means I look for things that I think are undervalued and invest in them. In the stock world, this usually - not always - means a low P/E and decent Dividend Yield. But the question I have to ask myself before I buy anything is, "Do I have a good, rational reason to buy it?"

What does this mean here? Well, it means that I have a bias. I do not think Bitcoin is a good investment idea right now in 2017. I do NOT think that Bitcoin is worth over $10K per coin. Some of that bias may come out. Lucky for you, though, this project isn't about that. I will not tell you to buy or not buy Bitcoin. That decision is yours to make.

I will, however, attempt to convince you that Bitcoin IRA, Inc. is a VERY BAD idea. for you if you decide to invest in Bitcoin. In addition, I will show you other, much less costly methods of doing so. Let's get started, shall we?

Problem #4: Clickbait

Near the bottom, I saw this article (link):

This article is crap. Yes, blockchain is becoming more widely accepted. The title, however, states that this "Hype Cycle" proves that Bitcoin is going to go up in value. Ok, look. When you prove something, you show objectively that it is true beyond any shadow of doubt. For example, you can prove that two shapes are congruent. You can prove that the Earth is not flat. You can prove that not everything that is true is provable. Applying this "Hype Cycle" here has several issues:

  • It provides no numbers and no timeframes.
  • It does not account for obsolescence of a technology. Remember the Laserdisc? The Betamax?
  • It doesn't match historical Bitcoin price history. There has always been skepticism surrounding it.
  • It doesn't account for the fact that the general public doesn't know anything useful about Bitcoin.
  • The adoption of technology is not mutually exclusive to overhyping.
  • It does not account for technologies that are accepted with little resistance, like the smartphone.
Tell me again how this subjective, overly simple model proves anything? All this article serves to do is tell you that Bitcoin is guaranteed to go up, therefore you should use Bitcoin IRA Inc. Never mind that so far, they haven't provided a single good reason for why I should use their services to invest in Bitcoin. I had to dig around to actually find it. The reasons they give are simplicity, no tax on capital gains, and security. I'll break these down next.

Problem #5: The Hidden Cost

So if all the deceptive practices listed above are not enough to convince you, let me ask you a question: How much is 15%? Why? Because Bitcoin IRA charges the following fees when you deposit your account:

  • 15% for 15k minimum to 100k
  • 13% for 100k to 200k
  • 11% for 200k and up

By the way, I didn't find this on their website, and believe me, I looked for it, even under the FAQs and Risk Disclosures pages. I had to find this out through a Google search, where I found this article.

Conclusion

All right. I think we're ready to answer those questions.

  • Are they convincing people to do this? If so, how? Yes, as evidenced by the Facebook and Google reviews. They have ads and spread deceptive information.
    • Who are they trying to market to? People close to the retirement age with fairly large retirement savings.
    • What are they telling the general public? See article.
    • What are they hiding? The very high charge on deposits and the speculative nature of BTC. Even though the latter is in the risks and disclosures page, all the "news" articles are trying to tell you that BTC is guaranteed to go up.
    • What's in it for them? They still profit even if BTC goes down or stays the same.
  • Is it a ruse, and if so, how elaborate of a ruse is it? I will not go so far as to call this organization fraudulent, I will say that its practices are very unethical. Pretty much everything on their homepage is meant to put up smoke and mirrors. Given a small sample of customers from their joke of a reviews section, it looks like they've duped quite a few innocent people.
